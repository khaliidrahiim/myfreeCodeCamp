Science-based Tools for ADHD

It is challenging to find trusted medical information to inform your behavioral health. This is why it's important to find and follow medical professionals who volunteer information for the societal benefit. Dr. Andrew Huberman is a professor of neurobiology and ophthalmology at Stanford School of Medicine who discusses science and science-based tools for everyday life on the Huberman Lab podcast. This page will introduce videos for managing ADHD.

Focus

Persons with ADHD, especially the inattentive type, have difficulty focusing. Though they can hyperfocus on things that interest them, their concentration tends to drift especially on tasks they don't want to complete. This video shares some tips on how you can improve your focus.

Pharmacology

Modern medicine offers many benefits for the diagnosed patient. A doctor's prescription is a godsend for managing the traits of inattention and/or hyperactivity. Medication alongside a healthy lifestyle gives you a good outlook on comfortably living with your diagnosis. This video discusses pharmaceutical support.

Behavioral Tools

A psychiatrist is the primary care physician of anyone who pharmacologically manages their behavioral health. In this video, Dr. Huberman interviews Dr. John Kruse, M.D., Ph.D., a psychiatrist specialising in treating people with ADHD.
