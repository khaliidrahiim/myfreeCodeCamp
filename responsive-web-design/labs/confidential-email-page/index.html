The Anegada Rock Iguana burrows can be found near salt ponds but above the water table. Naturally, they are rarely seen unless you go looking for them.

They are endangered but over 300 were released since the conservation programme started in the late '90s, so the population is likely increasing. However, environmental policy and public support are needed to secure the future of these reptiles.

The work done by Kelly Bradley and the Fort Worth Zoo has been crucial in preventing the extinction of the Rock Iguana. Still, the management of feral cats and other invasive species is key to saving the endemic iguana of The Virgin Islands.
