Utility merger gets guv's assent

Water and Sewerage Department and the BVI Electricity Corporation to merge into one statutory body.

by The BVI Beacon

The territory's electricity and water-and-sewerage utilities are set to merge after Governor Daniel Pruce assented to a law passed in March by the House of Assembly, officials said.

The Virgin Islands Energy and Water Corporation Act, 2026, will combine the Water and Sewerage Department and the BVI Electricity Corporation into one statutory body.

The governor's assent — which had not been published in the government's Gazette as of Beacon press time Wednesday afternoon — was announced June 4 by Communications and Works Minister Kye Rymer.

"This is a historic moment for the Virgin Islands,” Mr. Rymer said in a press release. “The merger reflects this government's commitment to building stronger, more resilient public institutions that can better serve our people today and for generations to come."

He added that the merged utility — the VI Energy and Water Corporation, which he abbreviated as “VIEWCo” — will boost efficiency, improve service delivery, and allow for more closely coordinated infrastructure planning and investment.
